About Rubik's Cube

                         

The Rubik's Cube is a 3D combination puzzle invented in 1974 by Hungarian sculptor and professor of architecture Ernő Rubik. Originally called the Magic Cube, it has become one of the world's best-selling toys and a popular puzzle for competitive speedcubing.

        
        
            

History of the Rubik's Cube

            

The Rubik's Cube was invented in 1974 by Hungarian architecture professor Ernő Rubik. Initially created as a teaching tool to help his students understand 3D objects, Rubik didn't realize he had created a puzzle until the first time he scrambled his new cube and tried to restore it. The cube was first called the "Magic Cube" in Hungary. Rubik applied for a patent in Hungary for the "Magic Cube" in 1975, and the first cubes were produced and sold by Politoys Ipari Szövetkezet in Budapest. The puzzle was licensed by Rubik to be sold by Ideal Toy Corp in 1980 and gained international popularity, becoming one of the world's best-selling toys. To date, over 350 million Rubik's Cubes have been sold worldwide, making it the best-selling toy of all time.

        
        
            

Types of Rubik's Cubes

Also known as the "Pocket Cube," this is a simpler version of the classic 3x3.

                
                
                                         

3x3 Cube

                    

The classic Rubik's Cube, with 43 quintillion possible combinations.

                
                
                                         

4x4 Cube

                    

Also called "Rubik's Revenge," this cube offers a greater challenge.
